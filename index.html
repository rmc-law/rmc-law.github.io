Research

My primary interest is how language constructs and conveys meaning. Some questions I find fascinating include: What is the nature of linguistic meaning representations? What is the underlying neurobiology and its relations with other brain systems (e.g. perceptual, motor, affective)? Recent interests include modelling meaning composition computationally. I also enjoy thinking about what scientists mean by 'mechanisms' or 'organisations'. Here are some recent projects:

Structured representations in the brain (with Liina Pylkkänen)

To form more complex meanings, word meanings combine in a structured manner. Can this structure be dissociated from specific word meanings? Using a novel design, we measured neural signals when people read using magnetoencephalography, and found that the frontotemporal system responded more strongly to structure even when words are the same. [preprint][poster]

Brain mechanisms in understanding modal expressions (with Maxime Tulling and Liina Pyllkänen)

Humans contemplate, make, and evaluate judgments about what is factual, possible and necessary. Language has grammatical devices for expressing possibilities and necessities, such as the words might or must. What are the brain correlates of possibility reasoning? When encoutering factual information, activity in the right temporo-parietal areas increased when we're updating our own beliefs; activity in the frontal medial areas increased when we're updating others' beliefs. [preprint]
